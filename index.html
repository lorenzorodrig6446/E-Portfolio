About Me

Where
Engineering
Meets Design

I'm a Aerospace Engineering student at The Pennsylvania State University, graduating May 2026 with a minor in Engineering Design and a GPA of 3.79. My academic and professional journey spans autonomous vehicle design, manufacturing process engineering, aerodynamics research, and software development — all united by a commitment to solving hard problems elegantly.

My goal is to contribute to the design, development, and refinement of some of the most complex propulsion systems in existence — systems like the F100 and F135 engines that push the boundaries of what aerospace engineering can achieve. These machines represent the intersection of precision manufacturing, thermodynamic performance, and systems-level thinking that I find deeply compelling.

What draws me to design is its inherent creative freedom within structure. In engineering, your solution must meet specifications, satisfy constraints, and pass rigorous validation — but how you get there is entirely up to you. That space between the requirements and the final design is where I do my best thinking. Every constraint is an invitation to be more creative, not less.
